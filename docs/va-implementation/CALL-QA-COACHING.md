## Overview

The Call QA & Coaching program ensures consistent, high-quality customer interactions that:

|------|--------|
| QA Score Average | ≥ 85% |
| Compliance Rate | 100% |

## Call Sampling Process

### Sample Size Requirements

| VA Experience | Calls/Week | Review Frequency |
|--------------|------------|------------------|
| New (0-30 days) | 5 calls | Daily reviews |
| Developing (30-90 days) | 3-5 calls | Twice weekly |
| Proficient (90+ days) | 3 calls | Weekly |
| Top Performer | 2 calls | Weekly spot check |

### Call Selection Criteria

**Stratified Random Sampling:**

1. **Call Types** (sample from each weekly):
   - Inbound inquiry calls
   - Outbound follow-up calls
   - Demo booking calls
   - Callback requests

2. **Call Outcomes** (include variety):
   - Successful conversions
   - Not interested outcomes
   - Voicemails left
   - Complex/escalated calls

3. **Duration Distribution**:
   - Short calls (< 2 min)
   - Standard calls (2-10 min)
   - Long calls (> 10 min)

### Accessing Call Recordings

1. Go to **GHL → Conversations → Phone**
2. Filter by:
   - Date range
   - User (VA)
   - Call direction
3. Click on call record
4. Play recording
5. Open QA scorecard in separate window

## Scoring Guidelines

### Scoring Rules

- **Yes (Full Points):** Criteria fully met
- **Partial (Half Points):** Criteria partially met
- **No (0 Points):** Criteria not met or incorrect
- **N/A:** Not applicable (adjust total accordingly)

### Examples: Opening & Professionalism

**3/3 - Professional Greeting:**
> ✅ "Thank you for calling Daily Event Insurance, this is Sarah. How can I help you today?"

**1.5/3 - Partial Credit:**
> ⚠️ "Daily Event Insurance, can I help you?"

**0/3 - No Credit:**
> ❌ "Hello?" or "Yeah?"

### Examples: Discovery

**5/5 - Uncovered Pain Points:**
> ✅ "So it sounds like your main concern is liability exposure for day-pass members who aren't covered by your general policy. Is that right?"

**2.5/5 - Partial:**
> ⚠️ Asked about current insurance but didn't dig into specific concerns

**0/5 - No Credit:**
> ❌ Jumped straight to product pitch without understanding needs

### Compliance Critical Failures

These result in automatic call failure (scored as 0% overall):

- Provided specific insurance advice
- Made coverage guarantees
- Ignored DNC request
- Shared confidential information
- Misrepresented role or product

## Feedback & Coaching

### Feedback Delivery Timeline

| Call Reviewed | Feedback Delivered By |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Monday | Wednesday |
| Tuesday | Thursday |
| Wednesday | Friday |
| Thursday-Friday | Monday |

### Feedback Session Structure (15-20 min)

1. **Open Positively (2 min)**
   - Thank them for their work
   - Mention one specific strength

2. **Review Together (5-7 min)**
   - Listen to key moments together
   - Ask for their self-assessment first
   - Discuss what went well

3. **Improvement Areas (5-7 min)**
   - Focus on 1-2 key areas max
   - Provide specific examples
   - Suggest concrete improvements
   - Role-play if helpful

4. **Action Items (3 min)**
   - Agree on specific goals
   - Set timeline for review
   - Document in coaching log

### Coaching Best Practices

✅ **DO:**
- Be specific and use examples
- Focus on behaviors, not personality
- Ask questions before telling
- Celebrate improvements
- Connect feedback to goals

❌ **DON'T:**
- Overwhelm with too many points
- Be vague ("Do better")
- Compare to other VAs by name
- Wait too long to provide feedback
- Focus only on negatives

## Calibration Sessions

### Purpose

Ensure all QA reviewers score consistently and fairly.

### Frequency

- **Initial Training:** 4-hour calibration
- **Ongoing:** Monthly 1-hour sessions
- **As Needed:** When new reviewers join

### Calibration Process

1. **Select 2-3 Calls** - Mix of quality levels
2. **Independent Scoring** - All reviewers score separately
3. **Compare Results** - Display all scores
4. **Discuss Variances** - Focus on > 5% differences
5. **Reach Consensus** - Agree on "correct" score
6. **Document Standards** - Update guidelines if needed

### Acceptable Variance

| Variance | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 0-5% | Acceptable |
| 6-10% | Discuss and align |
| 11%+ | Stop, recalibrate before continuing |

## Escalation & Support

### When to Escalate (QA Issues)

| Situation | Escalate To |
|-----------|-------------|
| Compliance violation | Team Lead + Compliance |
| Score < 60% | Team Lead |
| Pattern of decline | Team Lead |
| VA disputes score | Team Lead review |
| Training gap identified | Training Lead |

### Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Triggered when:
- Average QA < 70% for 2+ consecutive weeks
- Compliance failure
- Consistent failure to improve after coaching

**PIP Elements:**
1. Specific performance gaps identified
2. Measurable improvement goals
3. Timeline (typically 30 days)
4. Required training/support
5. Weekly progress reviews
6. Clear success criteria
